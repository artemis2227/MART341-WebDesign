About Me

What's a bio without an obligatory awkward life story? I promise to do my best to make this a little more interesting. It all started in 1980, when my father, then a stranger, climbed into my mother's dorm room windowsill. That sounds really creepy, but I guess it worked for her - 40 years later (38 of them married), Jeana and Kevin Van Nortwick have three adult children and four grandchildren. I am the youngest of the three children, a full 9 years younger than my sister. I was born in Palmer, Alaska and raised there for ten years. I've been a Christian all my life, having been raised in the church (good ones, filled with love- not hate). My life really picked up when we moved to the city of Anchorage at year 10. I went through a home-schooled middle school and private Christian high school before ending up at the University of Montana. Now, I'm in my final semester of my undergraduate degree, with plans to go on for a Master's degree in Clinical Psychology, to become a therapist. Over the years, the thing that shaped me the most were people, the good ones and the bad ones. I want to help teenagers going through difficult times, to be there for a kid not lucky enough to have the support I did. I just want to make a difference in one kids life; then it will be all worth it.
